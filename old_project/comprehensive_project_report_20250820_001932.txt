DETAILED DATASET ANALYSIS
==========================

ENERGY SYSTEM MONITORING CONTEXT:
The UK electricity grid operates as a complex real-time system where supply must continuously match demand to maintain stability. The grid frequency (target 50Hz) is the key indicator of balance - frequency drops when demand exceeds supply, rises when supply exceeds demand. These datasets monitor every aspect of this critical balancing act, from individual power station performance to market trading decisions to emergency responses.

PRIMARY ENERGY DATA (bmrs_data folder - 5.09 GB):

1. Bid-Offer Acceptances (BOD equivalent)
   - Files: ~2,409 daily files
   - Size: ~5.2 GB
   - Period: 2016-01-01 to present
   - Content: Daily trading decisions, bid acceptances, offer data
   - What it monitors: Power station bids to increase/decrease generation, grid operator acceptance decisions, balancing mechanism actions to maintain grid stability
   - Format: JSON files with detailed trading records
   - Path: bmrs_data/bid_offer_acceptances/YYYY/MM/

2. Demand Outturn
   - Files: ~2,406 daily files  
   - Size: ~0.4 MB
   - Content: Actual electricity demand vs forecasts
   - What it monitors: Real-time electricity consumption across the UK grid compared to predicted demand, showing accuracy of forecasting models
   - Path: bmrs_data/demand_outturn/

3. Generation Outturn
   - Files: ~2,406 daily files
   - Size: ~0.4 MB  
   - Content: Actual electricity generation by source
   - What it monitors: Real power output from all generation sources (gas, coal, nuclear, wind, solar, etc.) compared to scheduled generation plans
   - Path: bmrs_data/generation_outturn/

4. System Warnings
   - Files: ~182 files
   - Size: ~0.2 MB
   - Content: Grid stability and system alert data
   - What it monitors: Critical alerts about potential blackouts, equipment failures, supply shortages, or emergency grid conditions requiring immediate action
   - Path: bmrs_data/system_warnings/

ADDITIONAL DATASETS (datasets folder):
- FUELINST: Fuel mix and generation type data - Real-time monitoring of what fuel types (gas, coal, nuclear, renewables) are being used to generate electricity and their output levels
- INDGEN: Individual generator performance data - Performance metrics for each power station including output capacity, availability, and operational status
- MELNGC: Market index and pricing data - Electricity market pricing indices, marginal costs, and market clearing prices for different time periods
- MID: Market depth and liquidity indicators - Trading volume, bid-ask spreads, and market liquidity measurements for electricity trading
- NETBSAD: Network balancing services data - Grid balancing actions taken to maintain supply-demand equilibrium, including frequency response services
- TEMP: Temperature forecast data (2017+) - Weather temperature predictions used for electricity demand forecasting across different regions
- WINDFOR: Wind generation forecasts (2017+) - Predicted wind power generation output based on weather forecasts for wind farm planning
- FREQ: System frequency measurements - Real-time electricity grid frequency monitoring (target 50Hz in UK) indicating supply-demand balance
- SYSWARN: System warning notifications - Grid operator alerts about potential supply shortages, equipment outages, or system stability issues

DATASET INTRODUCTION TIMELINE
==============================

2016 Datasets (Base Year):
- FUELINST (Fuel Instruction) - Commands to power stations about which fuel types to use for generation optimization
- INDGEN (Individual Generation) - Performance monitoring of each power plant's output, efficiency, and availability status
- NETBSAD (Network Balancing) - Grid balancing services to maintain 50Hz frequency and voltage stability across the network
- NONBM (Non-Balancing Mechanism) - Generation and demand outside the main balancing mechanism (embedded generation, demand response)
- SYSWARN (System Warnings) - Critical grid operator alerts about supply risks, equipment outages, or emergency conditions
- MID (Market Index Data) - Electricity price indices reflecting marginal generation costs and market clearing prices
- DISBSAD (Balancing Services) - Disaggregated balancing services costs and volumes for grid stability maintenance
- QAS (Quality Assurance) - Data quality metrics and validation results for market data accuracy monitoring
- FREQ (Frequency Data) - Real-time grid frequency measurements (target 50Hz) indicating supply-demand balance

2017 Datasets:
- TEMP (Temperature Forecasts) - Weather temperature predictions used for electricity demand forecasting (heating/cooling demand correlation)
- WINDFOR (Wind Forecasts) - Predicted wind power generation output for renewable energy planning and grid balancing

2018+ Datasets (Priority Focus):
- BOD (Bid-Offer Data) - Available as bid_offer_acceptances - Power station trading bids and grid operator acceptance decisions for real-time balancing
- BOALF (Balancing Services Adjustment) - Adjustments to balancing mechanism actions and costs for grid frequency and voltage control
- MELNGC (Market Index) - Advanced market pricing indices and generation cost marginals for trading and settlement
